Digital Ineptitude, Or, Why I Couldn't Care Less About AI

For whom may be concerned,

As it customary, suffer me to open with a grand and obvious and broad and empty truism: Base 10 is the natural numerical base for humanity since we have 10 fingers (and toes, for the pedants).

Now observe the ease with which the natural articulation of joints in your hands permits you to touch, with the tip of your thumb, the 12 segments of your four non-opposable digits.

Now let go of value and units and count transitions: touch your thumb to the bottom segment of your index finger and count '1'; then, slide your thumb to the next joint for '2'; when you reach '3', reverse course and move back down the same finger for '4' and '5'; lastly, slide your thumb to the first segment of your middle finger for '6'. Each finger is a 5, each hand a score.

Can you see the action? Base 12 and base 20 are as natural as base 10. And if you have base 10 and base 12 and base 20, you also have base 60, which may be perhaps the best base. Just ask your clock. Hexidecimal is lurking around in there somewhere, two. But in any case, there are no natural bases, only spandrel bases.

Counting was praxis before theory, or, more simply, notation and taxonomy are foundational artifice. To understand is to be subjugated, the tempestuous primordial seas reduced to compassing notions surrounded by the flotsam and jetsam of what reason cannot navigate. Any craftsman so lazy as to let themselves be mastered by their tools loses their creative capacity. Generation != Creation. Philosophical banalities, surely, but no less consequential for that: the twinned scissor-blades of method and product divide the ecstatic signal from the sign's absurd vitality, cleave our mind, body, and soul from their animating motion and metabolism.

For any living spirit, cataclysm hides latent within the starvation of creativity and the dereliction of the poetic act.

A Feverish Hubris fuels the great engine of sloth gavaging the bright, cancerous blot of an illusory and incarcerating spandrel progress (so-called).

The praxes of modern science and engineering, now with their current linear-regression darlings, celebrate their conquering of trompe-l'oeil natures, their transmutation of gold into lead. All the while, the green lion is still eating the sun.

The artificial knows and the living improvises.

Colors appeared before our eyes in roughly the same sequence. Eye take back color from word.

Participation is mandatory.

Eric VanLieshout
